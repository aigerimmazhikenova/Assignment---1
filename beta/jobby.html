Nur-Sultan - the capital of the Republic Kazakhstan since December 10, 1997.
The city is located in the north of the country, on the banks of the Ishim River,
administratively divided into 4 districts. Akmolinsk received city status
on May 7, 1862. Astana became a millionaire city in June 2017, when the population was
1 002 874 inhabitants. To the beginning In 2021, the population of
Nur-Sultan was 1,184,469 people, which is the second indicator in Kazakhstan after Alma-Ata.

"Baiterek" (full name - "Astana-Baiterek"; kaz. Baiterek) is
a monument and observation tower in the capital of Kazakhstan Nur-Sultan. The project was launched on the
initiative of the first
president of the Republic of Kazakhstan, Nursultan Nazarbayev,
the project architect - Akmurza Rustembekov [1] [2]. During construction, the volume of
earthworks was 8600 m³,
concrete - 2900 m³, metal structures - 1 kiloton, dome glazing - 1555 m².
